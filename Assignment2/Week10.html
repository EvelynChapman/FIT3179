Buildings of the City of Melbourne

The City of Melbourne is a council area in the heart of Greater Melbourne, it is the capital of the state Victoria, Australia. It is comprised of a number of smaller areas including the CBD, Port Melbourne, Carlton, and many more. I have devided the uses of buildings into five catagories, Commercial, Public, Resedental, Reatal, and Unoccupied. From the map bellow left we can get an idea of how Melbournes suburbs differ in usage, with Central Melboure being a mix of everything but resedental, Port Melbourne to the south west being a comercal hub, and north western suburb of Ascot Vale being a resedental area.

A Historic City

The area of Melbourne was first settled in 1835 as modern Victroras first settlement becoming a town in 1842 and a city can capital of the Victoria colony in 1847. In the chart bellow we can see the distribution of age of buldings in melbourne as they remained in 2014, and we can see the limited number of buidings remain from this period with more apearing as the city grew into the 1870's.

Other patterns wecan pick up on include the drop is budings in the 1940, during the period of WWII period. a final observation is how the distribution of resedental buidling ages changes while most other types remain more consistant, likley due to single resedent houses being more likley to be redeveloped
